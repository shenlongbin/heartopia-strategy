What makes a useful guide site

The homepage should route people into the right content path instead of forcing them to read a giant wall of text.

  • Start here pages: beginner route, daily loop, key systems.
  • Lookup pages: resources, NPCs, map locations, unlock conditions.
  • Timely pages: bug workarounds, patch changes, missing features.
  • Internal links: turn broad guides into article clusters over time.

User needs

What players come here to solve

The most important content is the content that reduces friction right now. These four jobs should shape the early roadmap.

Onboarding

New players want a fast answer to “what should I do first?” and usually leave if they cannot find that answer quickly.

Routine

Daily checklist content creates repeat visits because players come back every session to confirm priorities.

Lookup

Resource and system pages support long-tail SEO and help the site grow beyond just a few broad guides.

Troubleshooting

Updates, bugs, and feature confusion drive urgent search traffic and make the site feel actively maintained.

Fresh content lanes

Keep the site useful between major guides

Not every page has to be a giant article. Smaller update and FAQ pages create trust and keep the content fresh.

Homepage FAQs

Questions worth answering fast on the front page

These questions can stay short here while also turning into dedicated content later.

What should I do first in Heartopia?

Point the user to a simple opening route with the first systems to unlock and early mistakes to avoid.

What are the best daily tasks?

Daily checklist content is one of the strongest return-visit hooks for this kind of site.

Where do I find important materials?

Resource lookup pages support long-tail search and usually become the biggest content cluster over time.

Did an update change or break something?

Timely update notes and workaround pages build trust because users can see the site is actively maintained.
